01
The problem 01 / 06
The packaging says $12 party product.
The ICP buys $79/mo AG1.
What the current brand signals
—
Cartoon smiley, party palette. Reads as a TikTok impulse buy — something you grab at a convenience store, not something you put in your gym bag next to your Momentous stack.
—
The mascot is doing the work the formula should do. 88 formulations. Netherlands lab. DHM, L-Theanine, Alpha GPC. That's a $250/mo supplement stack collapsed into one sachet. A dripping cartoon doesn't tell that story.
—
Visual language skews 18–22, male. The ICP is 50/50 M/F, 23–35, high earner. Female premium buyers don't buy supplements that look like energy drink merch.
—
"boolsalts" positions it as a salt product. The product is a nootropic stack that hydrates. The name buries the lead — and the nootropic angle is the only differentiator no competitor can match.
What the ICP needs to see
+
Clinical confidence without the white coat. They read ingredient labels. They know what L-Theanine does. The brand should feel like it knows too — without having to explain everything at every touchpoint.
+
Premium shelf presence. This person buys On Running, Whoop, AG1. They make purchase decisions by feel before they read the label. The pack needs to earn the shelf spot on sight.
+
Gender-neutral visual language. Not girl-pink or bro-black. Sharp geometry, restrained palette, real typography — something that feels like a precision tool, not a product line.
+
Own the nootropic angle. No electrolyte brand owns cognitive hydration. L-Theanine + Alpha GPC → alpha-wave clarity. That's a product story no competitor can claim. It's the entire wedge.
Keep the irreverence. Kill the cartoon. The personality lives in the voice — not the mascot.
The founder's wit, the "comeback mode," the obsession with the formula — those all stay. The dripping smiley doesn't represent any of that. "High output hydration" does. That's the rebrand in one sentence.
02
Founder's mandate 02 / 06
"Investment banker. But not too serious.
Not Equinox."
The brief in a sentence. The brand needs to hold two things at once — and most brands pick one. This is what it looks like to land in the middle.
Not this
Equinox mode
Cold prestige. You feel the velvet rope before you open the product. Aspirational from the outside, gatekeeping from the inside. The brand lectures you about your potential and takes itself too seriously to have a personality.
AG1 late era · SoulCycle · Lululemon-austere · most premium supplement brands
Not this either
Liquid Death mode
Maximum irreverence, zero substance. The personality IS the product — works when you're selling water. When the formula has receipts, leading with the bit first is a credibility drain. The joke can't outlast the ingredient panel.
Liquid Death · Bang Energy · GFuel · Alani Nu
The zone
Confident swagger.
You know what's in it. You know why it works. You're not going to explain it at every touchpoint — but you're also not above making a joke. Science that doesn't announce itself. Personality that doesn't need to perform.
LMNT voice · Erewhon culture · On Running visual language
The ICP's gym bag
On Running shoes. Whoop band. AG1 shaker. Momentous creatine. They'll spend $200/mo on supplements without blinking — if the product earns it visually at shelf. The brand has one shot at that first impression.
Their Friday
Out 2× a week. Not a club rat, not home by 10. The PM SKU (passionfruit mango + DHM) plays directly into this — go hard without the next-day tax. That's a real use case. A confident brand owns it without winking too hard.
Why they tell people
They try once on vibe. They reorder because it works. They tell people because it makes them look like they know something others don't. The punchline has to be the formula. Not the mascot.

Competitive reads
LMNT
Premium, clinical, austere. Ingredient-first. No cognitive angle. Their ICP overlaps directly — but they're a salt brand, not a brain brand. Bool can be both.
Liquid IV
Mass-market glucose hydration. The EL SKU beats LIV on formula via the SGLT1 pathway + full electrolyte stack. LIV wins on shelf presence — that closes when the rebrand happens.
Thesis / Momentous
Premium nootropics and sports nutrition. Their buyers are exactly bool's ICP. Nobody in that space is also a hydration brand. This is the crossover nobody has done.
The gap
Premium + personality + cognitive hydration. No brand owns alpha-wave focus, 3pm crash recovery, and hangover science simultaneously. The formula is already there. The brand needs to say it.
